The potential good effects of PHC support both improved patient result and a more efficient use of the resources of healthcare. These include, but are not restricted to:

1.Decreased trial-and-error medicine and potential adverse drug reactions

(Hover to read more)

(a). Provide diagnostic tests to help identify patients who will probably respond to a specific treatment (eg. companion diagnostics)
(b). Help in the development of better treatments

2. Increased speed and accuracy of diagnosis

(Hover to read more)

(a). Allow more accurate diagnostics of patients with the need of earlier and better treatment.

3. Increased provision of preventive healthcare

(Hover to read more)

(a). Identify patients who are more at a risk of a particular disease and help them with the preventive measures that can be taken

4. Increased understanding of disease

(Hover to read more)

(a). Understand the important factors to disease embodiment and sequence
(b). Develop and provide better treatments, diagnoses and testing procedures to the patients.

5. Decreased time, burden and cost of drug development

(Hover to read more)

(a). Foretell pharmacogenetics in advance with simulations
(b). Allow for scientifically useful trials on smaller patient groups who are qualified by specific biosignatures
